This Sunday, March 8

Resolved, in Order to provide for the common Appetite, promote the general Welfare, and secure the Blessings of Beef to ourselves and our Posterity, we do ordain and establish this Manifesto for Meat Club.

~ ~ ~

Article I — The Sacred Covenant

Whereas certain Persons do gather monthly to discuss Literature and consume Cheeses of French and Continental origin, and whereas their Partners and chosen Friends do find themselves without Purpose during these Gatherings, it is hereby resolved that a separate Assembly shall convene, dedicated to the Preparation and Consumption of Meats.

This Assembly shall be known as Meat Club.

Article II — On the Meats

All Meats are welcome within this Assembly. None shall be turned away, for we hold these Truths to be self-evident: that all Meats are created equal, though some meats are more equal than others.

Article III — On the Time of Gathering

Whereas the Book Club doth gather, so too shall Meat Club assemble. While they discuss books, we shall discuss liquid smoke and preheating. While they consume Brie, we shall consume Beef.

Article IV — On the Conduct of Members

No Member shall smoke, brine, dry-rub, or otherwise prepare any Rack of Pork Ribs without first removing the Pleura, that being the thin subcutaneous Membrane affixed to the posterior Side of said Ribs. Neither shall the Silverskin be left intact upon any Cut intended for the Low and Slow, for it shall render the Bark uneven and the Bite unpleasant.

Furthermore, the right of the Pitmaster to spatchcock, french, or chine any Bird or Rack shall not be infringed, provided the Pellicle be allowed to form in refrigerated Rest before the Application of Smoke.

Whereas Meat Club exists in parallel to more refined Assemblies, Members are encouraged to engage in minor Acts of Mischief and Mayhem, including but not limited to: the telling of tall Tales, the playing of Games involving Chance and Probability, the hiding of the good Napkins, and the occasional Exaggeration of one's grilling Accomplishments.

Such Mischief shall be conducted in good Spirit and shall not rise to the level of actual Malfeasance.

Article V — On Randomness and Heavy Tails

Members shall cultivate an Appreciation for the Pareto Distribution and other heavy-tailed Phenomena, wherein the Majority of outcomes are modest but a precious Few are spectacular beyond Measure. So it is with Hands dealt, and with Briskets smoked.

Let no Member mistake the Median for the Mean, nor dismiss the Possibility of Outliers.

~ ~ ~

Signed in Grease and Solidarity,

The Founding Members of Meat Club.
