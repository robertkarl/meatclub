This Sunday

Resolved, in Order to form a more perfect Union of hungry Men, to establish Fellowship, ensure domestic Satisfaction, provide for the common Appetite, promote the general Welfare, and secure the Blessings of Smoked Meats to ourselves and our Posterity, we do ordain and establish this Manifesto for Meat Club.

~ ~ ~

Article I — The Sacred Covenant

Whereas certain Persons do gather monthly to discuss Literature and consume Cheeses of French and Continental origin, and whereas their Partners and chosen Friends do find themselves without Purpose during these Gatherings, it is hereby resolved that a separate Assembly shall convene, dedicated to the Preparation and Consumption of Meats.

This Assembly shall be known as Meat Club, and its Purpose shall be noble.

Article II — On Discourse

The first Rule of Meat Club shall be thus: one always speaks of Meat Club. Let it be known throughout the Land. Let the Smoke signal our Presence. Let the Neighbors wonder, and let them envy.

Article III — On the Meats

All Meats are welcome within this Assembly. None shall be turned away, for we hold these Truths to be self-evident: that all Meats are created equal, though some require longer Smoking than others.

The following Meats are recognized and celebrated:

  • Brisket, the undisputed King
  • Ribs, beloved of the People
  • Pulled Pork, the democratic Choice
  • Burger, humble yet essential
  • Steak, for when Words fail
  • Sausage, the unsung Hero
  • Lamb, for the Adventurous
  • Whole Hog, for Days of particular Glory

Let it be known: no Meat is too ambitious, and no Portion too large.

Article IV — On the Time of Gathering

Whereas the Book Club doth gather, so too shall Meat Club assemble. While they read, we shall smoke. While they discuss Themes and Symbolism, we shall discuss Bark and Smoke Rings. While they consume Brie, we shall consume Beef.

This is the Way, and it shall not be altered.

Article V — On the Conduct of Members

Be it resolved that every Member of this Assembly shall:

  1. Bring something to the Fire or to the Table
  2. Respect the Pitmaster's Vision
  3. Never depart whilst Hungry
  4. Take Nap if needed, without Shame or Apology
  5. Speak of the Meats consumed with Reverence in the Days following

Article VI — On Forbidden Acts

The following Actions are hereby prohibited and shall be considered contrary to the Spirit of this Assembly:

  1. Suggesting the Assembly "do a Salad instead"
  2. Departing early whilst Meat remains
  3. Disrespecting another Man's Rub Recipe
  4. Introducing Veggie Burgers without prior Authorization
  5. Checking the Time

Article VII — On the Eternal Flame

Meat Club is more than Meat. It is Brotherhood. It is Tradition. It is standing around a Grill saying "that looks about ready" no fewer than six Times before it is actually ready.

Meat Club is forever.

~ ~ ~

Signed in Grease and Solidarity,

The Founding Members of Meat Club

Established whilst our Partners discussed Books and consumed Fancy Cheese
